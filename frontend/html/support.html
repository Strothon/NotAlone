    Institutions to Apply for Support

    A) ORGANIZATIONS AFFILIATED WITH THE MINISTRY OF FAMILY AND SOCIAL POLICIES

    You can contact the Provincial and District Directorates of the Ministry of Family, Labor and Social Services by phone or in person to get information about the nearest Social Service Center, Violence Prevention and Monitoring Center (ŞÖNİM) and other relevant counseling centers for violence-related counseling support.

    The addresses and contact information of women's shelters affiliated with the Ministry of Family, Labor and Social Services are not public due to confidentiality principles. You can reach the necessary information by calling the ALO 183 Line and the Violence Prevention and Monitoring Centers (ŞÖNİM) listed below.

    By calling ALO 183, which provides free service 24/7, you can access information about the nearest Social Service Center, Family Counseling Center, Violence Prevention and Monitoring Center (ŞÖNİM) and women's shelters.

    C) WOMEN'S SHELTERS AND REFUGES

    Shelters aim to provide refuge and protection for all women subjected to domestic violence, support women in building a new life in solidarity with other women, direct them to relevant institutions, help them gain awareness of their rights against violence, and build self-confidence. Shelters support women and their children who have been subjected to violence by providing social, psychological, and legal support, helping them establish a new life away from violence. According to the statement made by the Ministry of Family and Social Services in July 2020, there are 145 women's shelters across the country, including 110 women's shelters affiliated with the Ministry of Family and Social Services in 81 provinces, 2 women's shelters affiliated with the Directorate General of Migration Management, and 1 women's shelter affiliated with a non-governmental organization, serving with a total capacity of 3,482 beds. If you need to stay in a women's shelter, you can apply to the Violence Prevention and Monitoring Center (ŞÖNİM) in your province, the Provincial or District Directorates of the Ministry of Family and Social Services, the police station/gendarmerie command, women's counseling centers of municipalities, and/or other relevant service units and women's organizations.
